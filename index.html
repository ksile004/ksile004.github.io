My name is Karen Silerio, and I am passionate about empowering young people and fostering positive change in our communities. With a background in youth development and community engagement, I've dedicated my professional career to creating opportunities for growth and success among today's youth.My journey in youth advocacy began with my tenure at the Young People Project, where I had the privilege of working directly with young individuals from diverse backgrounds. Through mentorship, guidance, and advocacy, I helped them navigate challenges and unlock their full potential. Whether it was providing academic support, facilitating leadership workshops, or organizing extracurricular activities, my goal was always to instill confidence and inspire a sense of purpose in the youth I served.

My journey in community activism began during my high school years when I served as the President of the 12th-grade class. In this role, I spearheaded various initiatives aimed at fostering inclusivity, promoting student voice, and addressing issues such as bullying and discrimination. One of my proudest accomplishments was leading a young people's project dedicated to combating anti-social behavior (ASB) in our school and surrounding neighborhoods.

Throughout my tenure, I worked closely with fellow students, faculty members, and community leaders to develop innovative strategies for addressing ASB and promoting a culture of respect and understanding. From organizing awareness campaigns to facilitating dialogue sessions, I was committed to creating safe and supportive environments where every individual felt valued and heard.
